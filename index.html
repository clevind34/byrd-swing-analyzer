iPhone Camera Setup

Best settings for swing video
Settings > Camera > Record Video:
Select 4K at 120fps (or 1080p at 240fps if you want more frames to work with). The iPhone 17 supports both. 120fps at 4K is the sweet spot for swing analysis — sharp frames with enough resolution to see hand position and barrel angle.

Settings > Camera > Record Slo-mo:
Set to 1080p at 240fps. Use Slo-mo mode if you want to scrub through the swing in the Photos app before uploading. The app will extract the full-speed frames either way.

Lock exposure and focus:
Before recording, tap and hold on Gavin in the frame until you see AE/AF Lock appear. This prevents the camera from hunting for focus mid-swing, which causes blur on the barrel.
Camera position
Best angle: Directly to the side (perpendicular), at hip height. If you're filming from the 1B or 3B side, get far enough back to see the full swing without panning.

Distance: About 10-15 feet away. Close enough to see hand and hip detail, far enough to capture the full body.

Tripod: Use one if you have it. A steady camera makes frame extraction way better. Even a phone clamp on a fence works.

Lighting: Face the light source. Don't shoot into the sun or stadium lights. The cage is usually fine. For outdoor games, shoot from the side where the light is behind you.
Quick recording checklist
✓ 4K / 120fps or Slo-mo 240fps
✓ AE/AF Lock on the hitter
✓ Side angle, hip height
✓ 10-15 feet away, no panning
✓ Light behind you, not behind the hitter
✓ Start recording before the pitch, stop after follow-through

Pre-cage warmup
Separation Freeze 15 reps
Stride and freeze. Don't swing. Check: hips starting to open, front shoulder still pointed at the catcher. If your shoulder opened with your hips, reset.
Focus: Feel the stretch across your midsection at foot strike
Med Ball Wall Throws 3 x 8
Stand sideways to a wall. Load back, throw a 6-8 lb med ball into the wall by rotating hips first, arms follow. If your arms are doing the work, slow down.
Focus: Hips start the throw, arms just come along for the ride
Cage work
Post-Up Tee Work 20 swings
Only focus: front leg firm at contact like a post. Hold your finish 2 seconds. If your front knee is soft or you're falling forward, that's energy leaking.
Focus: Firm front leg, balanced finish you can hold
